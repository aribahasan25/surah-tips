Quran - Surahs 91 to 100

91. Ash-Shams (The Sun)

Meaning: Oaths by creation; contrast of purified and corrupted souls; story of Thamud.

92. Al-Lail (The Night)

Meaning: Contrast between generous and miserly; guidance towards ease or hardship.

93. Ad-Duha (The Morning Hours)

Meaning: Allah’s reassurance to the Prophet (ﷺ); encouragement to care for orphans and needy.

94. Ash-Sharh (The Relief)

Meaning: Consolation to the Prophet (ﷺ); with hardship comes ease; reminder to focus on Allah.

95. At-Tin (The Fig)

Meaning: Human creation in the best form, downfall when denying faith, and certainty of Judgment.

96. Al-‘Alaq (The Clot)

Meaning: The first revelation; importance of reading, knowledge, and obedience to Allah.

97. Al-Qadr (The Power)

Meaning: The Night of Decree; worship in it is better than a thousand months.

98. Al-Bayyina (The Clear Proof)

Meaning: The Messenger and the clear evidence; difference between believers and disbelievers.

99. Az-Zalzalah (The Earthquake)

Meaning: Earthquakes on Judgment Day; people will see every deed, big or small.

100. Al-‘Adiyat (The Courser)

Meaning: Oath by war horses; human ingratitude; reminder of resurrection.
